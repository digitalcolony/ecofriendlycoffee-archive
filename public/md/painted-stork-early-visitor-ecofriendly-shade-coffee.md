# Painted Stork – An Early Visitor To Ecofriendly Shade Coffee [Biodiversity](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/category/biodiversity/)---by [Dr. Anand Titus and Geeta N. Pereira](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/author/dr-anand-titus-and-geeta-n-pereira/) Shade Grown Ecofriendly Coffee forests are often referred to as Bird sanctuaries by foreigners for the simple reason that one can hear and see thousands of song birds and other species of birds nest inside shade coffee. The Coffee forest provides the right environment for a variety of both resident and migratory birds.![2015 PS SLIDE-2](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-2-1024x683.CbLf6itK_Z20zJ3R.webp)![2015 PS SLIDE-34](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-34-1024x683.CZuoCOKu_cNuzT.webp) Migration can happen right in your own backyard. This simple article provides an opportunity for bird lovers inside shade coffee in the Districts of Hassan, Coorg and Chikmagalur to witness the arrival of the spectacularly colored, Painted Stork, a  winter visitor in  their very own back yard. Painted Storks are very good in adapting to new surroundings but require wetlands and marsh lands.![2015 PS SLIDE-3](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-3-1024x683.DogaQOji_Zvy3mo.webp)![2015 PS SLIDE-30](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-30-1024x683.CxSMrTTZ_2wO1Us.webp) ### **Description**![2015 PS SLIDE-31](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-31-1024x683.DTAKiFPp_uB0J.webp) The Painted Stork also known as Indian wood stork is a brightly colored bird with striking wing patterns. The male tends to have a larger body and bill than the female. The height of the bird is approximately 93-102 cm and weight is around 2 to 3.5kg.![2015 PS SLIDE-6](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-6-1024x683.vX_cRkqi_Z2gjrAi.webp)![2015 PS SLIDE-12](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-12-1024x683.C-6PzkQw_1sgKUW.webp) The young are not as colorful as the adult. The adults have a long yellow orange bill and pink legs. They display white plumage with a rose color near the tail feathers. Non-breeding plumage is usually less vibrant than breeding plumage. Juveniles are pale brown lacking a pectoral band.![2015 PS SLIDE-35](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-35-1024x683.DVHJEodi_ZuAgJ5.webp) ### **Male and Female** Males and females are not sexually dimorphic. Both the male and the female look alike.![2015 PS SLIDE-7](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-7-1024x683.BAnhONT7_ZR0wpd.webp)![2015 PS SLIDE-14](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-14-1024x640.E4xYn_AH_ZAI8e9.webp) ### **Scientific Classification**![2015 PS SLIDE-9](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-9-1024x683.C056OojE_ZCOgCp.webp)![2015 PS SLIDE-6A](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-6A-1024x683.-PitZ-et_YoGsM.webp)**Kingdom** Animalia**Phylum** Chordata**Class** Aves**Order** Ciconiformes**Family** Ciconiidae**Genus** Mycteria**species** Mycteria leucocephala![2015 PS SLIDE-8](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-8-1024x683.WI5LvFz0_22ecu.webp) ### **Distribution/Range**![2015 PS SLIDE-11](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-11-1024x683.DqSCyusF_WUs9C.webp) The Painted Stork has a wide range in the Indian Sub Continent. Painted storks are predominately non-migratory and most make only local movements. However some birds have been known to migrate to west Burma.![2015 PS SLIDE-10](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-10-1024x683.jM5Z38Yt_ZtE4CX.webp) Despite this fact, year on year we have witnessed less number of painted storks visiting coffee Plantations. A decade back, this bird was considered a widespread bird, but now the numbers have significantly declined.![2015 PS SLIDE-22](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-22-1024x683.vwLeeqGW_Z2fEvGn.webp) ### Breeding/Nesting Painted storks are a monogamous species. The mating season coincides with the latter part of the rainy season, starting from August in the North of its range and November to March in the South of its range. The male chooses a nest site and defends its territory.![2015 PS SLIDE-33](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-33-1024x683.Dxwh59oD_Z2myLK2.webp) The females preferably select a larger male. Once the male and the female are paired they both help in building the nest. The female painted stork lays between two and five eggs which are incubated for about a month, with both the male and female taking terns at incubation.![2015 PS SLIDE-16](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-16-1024x683.DZe2d2NJ_7eIHH.webp) Each parent will take turns feeding nestlings until they fledge. Both male and female painted storks share responsibilities when incubating and raising young. The young are born altricial, without feathers and with eyes closed. Young ones fly when they are 60 days old and reach maturity at 4 years of age.![2015 PS SLIDE-29](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-29-1024x626.D5bFbYpS_Z22r4D8.webp) The painted stork is a colonial nester. It is very common to see hundreds of nests on a single tree. These birds are often found nesting along with herons, Ibises and storks.  Research data shows that painted storks often return to breed in the same tree year after year. ### Food Habits![2015 PS SLIDE-21](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-21-1024x683.D5BjiBh9_Z2w8bbp.webp) Painted storks have been known to feed on fish, insects, earthworms, crustaceans, amphibians and reptiles.![2015 PS SLIDE-18](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-18-1024x683.DhP5X7IH_Z1AAJ2l.webp) ### **Predation** Painted storks are predated by human beings. ### **Threats**![2015 PS SLIDE-26.](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-26.-1024x644.DY_dRQoq_ZDJmbe.webp) Intensification of agriculture and conversion of wetlands into areca nut and oil palm is severely impacting the food resources, especially during refueling stops. The indiscriminate use of herbicides and weedicides, and pesticides is also a direct threat.![2015 PS SLIDE-25.](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-25..Cwkcj1Bu_Z1PtRCh.webp) ### **Calls/Vocalizations** We have not recorded any calls. Storks have no syrinx and are mute. They give no bird call. Bill clattering is an important mode of communication between and among storks.![2015 PS SLIDE-20](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-20-1024x683.BMuaOPPr_dKB10.webp) ### **Habitat**![2015 PS SLIDE-5](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-5-1024x683.BU9n3b_s_Z8YDUG.webp) It is pretty common to see painted storks inside shade grown ecofriendly coffee plantations where there is very less of human disturbance. Generally, these beautiful Birds prefer to habitat isolated ponds and lakes but are also quite comfortable in foraging in wetlands during the daytime, despite the presence of human activity.![2015 PS SLIDE-13A](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-13A.CkcyHh5K_NNhRH.webp) ### **Painted stork status**![2015 PS SLIDE-4A](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-4A-1024x683.D4662QC7_Z1bq1zx.webp) Painted storks have been classified as near threatened by the IUCN Red list of Threatened species and their population continues to decline throughout the coffee growing belts. Although one of the most abundant of the Asian storks, this species is classified as Near Threatened by the IUCN Red list of Threatened species and their population continues to be undergoing a moderately rapid population decline owing primarily to hunting, wetland drainage , pollution, deforestation and land fragmentation.![2015 PS SLIDE-27.](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-27..CaThXCGo_1qQScp.webp) ###  Conclusion Today, with birds threatened as never before, we need to redouble our efforts to protect bird habitats. It is evident from the declining numbers of bird species that birds cannot adjust to man. Instead, man should adjust to them. As we have earlier mentioned, painted stork’s numbers are declining inside shade coffee.![2015 PS SLIDE-17](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-17-1024x683.r_I1aEOA_29oQMB.webp) In our personal opinion the single most important factor contributing to this decline is the conversion of wetlands into oil palm and areca nut plantations. This has a direct impact on the availability of food for these birds that generally prefer aquatic and semi aquatic habitats.![2015 PS SLIDE-19](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-19-1024x683.DJZGijCO_2gcQYT.webp) Both Resident and Migratory bird populations inside shade coffee are in sharp decline year after year due to habitat loss. We need to redouble our efforts to conserve wetlands and marsh lands so that we directly encourage these beautiful winged visitors to be a part of our coffee landscape.![2015 PS SLIDE-24](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-24-1024x683.DeGDUgE__1WHveK.webp)![2015 PS SLIDE-28](https://ecofriendlycoffee.org/assets/2015-PS-SLIDE-28-1024x683.D98dtCeV_bFL2R.webp) ### References Anand T Pereira and Geeta N Pereira. 2009.
