Shade grown Ecofriendly Coffee Plantations house a wealth of biodiversity because the entire stretch of coffee forests support different specialized habitats, both micro and macro for all types of flora and fauna.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (1)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-1-1024x576_f_improf_604x340.jpg)

This is evident from our work spanning three decades, mapping the biodiversity of coffee forests in different agro climatic regions.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (2)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-2-1024x683_f_improf_604x403.jpg)

This article is unique because, for the very first time we have tried to study the behaviour of spiders.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (3)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-3-1024x683_f_improf_604x403.jpg)

Spiders are believed to inhabit almost every coffee Agro climatic region without any exception.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (4)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-4-1024x743_f_improf_604x438.jpg)

Spiders are relatively versatile and they do well in all types of habitat, irrespective of altitude and temperature differentials.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (5)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-5-1024x683.jpg)

They are of great value to the multi diversity coffee environment.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (6)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-6-1024x683.jpg)

The order Araneida contains the spiders. We presume they are generally beneficial, since they feed mainly upon insects.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (7)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-7-1024x939.jpg)

We are exploring the possibilities of using spiders as biological control agents in the reduction of pest populations.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (8)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-8-1024x828.jpg)

The essential difference between biological control and natural control is merely that in biological control natural enemies are encouraged and disseminated by man.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (9)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-9-1024x683.jpg)

It is important to understand that almost all biological control programs involve searching for parasites and predators of an introduced pest in the original habitat of the pest.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (10)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-10-1024x683.jpg)

We have documented various spider species. They come in all shapes and sizes. Some are big, some are small and others are microscopic.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (11)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-11-1024x683.jpg)

The colours too vary. A few are brightly coloured and a few are pale in colour.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (12)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-12-1024x683.jpg)

**Coffee Plantation Ecology**

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (14)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-14-1024x786.jpg)

This article highlights the role of spiders and their importance in food chains, food webs and most importantly balancing the web of life. Spiders have a profound impact on the environment they live in.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (13)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-13-1024x683.jpg)

We have monitored spider webs in all coffee blocks and are of the opinion that the natural habitat of spiders generally consists of its food sources in the given natural surroundings.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (15)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-15-1024x683.jpg)

One needs to understand that the coffee ecosystem is pretty complex and can provide multiple environments from one coffee block to the other.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (17)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-17-1024x683.jpg)

However, the elevation where coffee is cultivated plays a significant role in the establishment of spider populations.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (16)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-16-1024x576.jpg)

In short, topography and climate play a crucial role in the build up of spider populations.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (18)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-18-1024x737.jpg)

Different species thrive well at an elevation of 800 metres right up to 1250 metres main sea level.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (19)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-19-1024x599.jpg)

We have not studied in detail the different spider species but our observations point out that each species of spider has evolved to occupy and adapt a specialized ecological niche and are capable of tolerating variations in climate.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (20)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-20-1024x683.jpg)

The behavior of each species may vary depending on a host of factors like topography, cultural practices, type of mixed cropping and variety of heterogeneous tree populations.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (21)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-21-1024x674.jpg)

The shape and size of the spider webs varies from species to species. Some species, for instance, prefer a spider habitat that allows for the building of simple webs laid out on the coffee bush and others build a funnel shaped web on coffee leaf litter.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (22)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-22-1024x708.jpg)

Some others build large and complex net like webs attached to trees.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (23)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-23-1024x562.jpg)

In particular the ability of spiders to survive in huge numbers on moderately little food gives them an edge in maintaining viable populations even in extreme environments and this single factor in adaptability can play a pivotal role in using spiders as bio control agents.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (24)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-24-1024x683.jpg)

We have come across literature where some spiders are being promoted in the battle against agricultural pests. Linyphiids against aphids in wheat fields in the UK and *Pardosa ramulosa* in California where it has been shown that 20 spiders per square metre can reduce leaf hoppers in rice fields by up to 90%.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (25)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-25-1024x683.jpg)

We do hope our preliminary work on spiders as biological control agents will enthuse the Research wing of the Coffee Board and they in turn can conduct experimental trials on the role of spiders in controlling pest populations.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (26)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-26-1024x975.jpg)

**Observations with respect to Spider Behaviour**

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (27)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-27-1024x781.jpg)

Spider population is very high in coffee forest blocks where composting is regularly applied.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (28)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-28-1024x755.jpg)

Drastic reduction in spider population in blocks where chemical fertilizers are applied.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (29)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-29-1024x576.jpg)

Maximum population observed during the months of June to January.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (30)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-30-1024x683.jpg)

Coffee blocks close to virgin forests have high population of different spider species.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (31)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-31-1024x746.jpg)

Nill or minimum spider species and their populations in coffee blocks where weedicides and chemical sprays are regularly applied.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (32)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-32-1024x683.jpg)Very high spider population and species diversity in blocks having heterogeneous tree population.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (33)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-33-1024x683.jpg)

Species diversity in coffee blocks interspersed with multiple cropping like oranges, cardamom, pepper, arecanut , Robusta and Arabica species.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (34)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-34-1024x683.jpg)

Population trends can be reversed. ie. In blocks where regular application of chemical fertilizers is practiced and having statically insignificant spider populations, customary application of compost will help in build up of population but will take time (2 to 3 years).

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (35)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-35-1024x683.jpg)

Our observations point out that there are different species of spiders occupying different ecological niches right from underground dwelling spiders to tree top dwelling spiders.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (36)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-36-1024x556.jpg)

Coffee blocks adjoining paddy fields, streams, rivulets and water bodies have a sizeable population all year round.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (37)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-37-1024x683_f_improf_604x403.jpg)

Depending on the habitat, our studies have clearly pointed out that ground dwelling spiders are not territorial but can co habit with similar species at close distances.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (40)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-40-1024x576.jpg)

Tree dwelling spider populations are indeed territorial and generally have a safe distance of 500 metres or more from each other.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (39)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-39-1024x683.jpg)

We were unable to correlate the population dynamics of spiders, the number of species with the health of coffee ecosystem. In simple words, coffee blocks having high population of spiders need not necessarily be free of pest and diseases.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (38)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-381-1024x576.jpg)

Spiders which had their web exposed at a height of 10 to 12 feet between tree branches were untouched by birds or other prey.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (41)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-41-1024x683.jpg)We have noticed that most of the spider species inside coffee blocks eat all types of insects and not only pests. ie. They eat both beneficial and harmful insects.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (42)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-42-1024x625.jpg)

**Advantages of Biological Control**

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (43)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-43-576x1024.jpg)

Biological control has the potential to keep the pest population below the level of economic damage.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (44)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-44-1024x683.jpg)

If Biological control is successful, it becomes self sustaining and integrated into the normal environment of the control area.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (45)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-45-1024x683.jpg)

Biological control tends to be particularly useful on multi crops where complete control may not be required or where chemicals are not required.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (46)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-46-1024x683.jpg)

Leaves behind no toxic residue or chemicals in the food chain.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (47)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-47-1024x683.jpg)

**Disadvantages of Biological Control**

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (48)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-48-1024x683.jpg)

Establishment of insect control agents of sizeable populations is no easy task.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (49)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-49-1024x683.jpg)

Even if the beneficial insect populations are multiplied in the laboratory, there is no guarantee that they will adapt to the new environment.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (50)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-50-1024x683.jpg)

Takes time for the released insect to establish and stabilize and act on the pathogen.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (51)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-51-1024x664.jpg)

Degree of control has not often been obtained to the farmers satisfaction

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (52)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-52-1024x683.jpg)

Consumers often look for a perfect product on the supermarket shelf and the farmer resorts to chemical control where by the biological control agent is killed in the process

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (53)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-53-1024x683.jpg)

 **Conclusion**

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (54)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-54-1024x776.jpg)

Worldwide more and more consumers are aware of the dangers of residues of synthetic chemicals in foods and natural beverages.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (55)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-55-1024x722.jpg)

Off late, due to the impact of global warming, Pesticide residues in coffee are on the increase at an alarming rate.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (56)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-56-1024x683.jpg)

The European Union and the U.S. Department of Agriculture has laid out stringent guidelines on the permissible limits of the presence of chemicals to safeguard health.

![2015 slide-1 Dr.GNP. Ecofriendlycoffee (57)](2015-slide-1-Dr.GNP_.-Ecofriendlycoffee-57-1024x683.jpg)

One way of tackling this problem is by using insects as biocontrol agents.
