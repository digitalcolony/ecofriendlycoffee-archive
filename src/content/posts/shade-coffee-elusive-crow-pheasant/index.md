The great or common Coucal is commonly referred to as the crow pheasant in India.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  4](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-4-1024x682_f_improf_604x402.jpg)

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  2](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-2-1024x721.jpg)

Even though, it is present in different coffee  Agro Climatic conditions, it is difficult to spot the bird because it is extremely shy of humans.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  3](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-3-1024x682.jpg)

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  1](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-1-1024x670_f_improf_604x395.jpg)

### Other Common Names

Common Coucal, Large Coucal, Common Crow-pheasant, Lark-heeled Cuckoo

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  13](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-13-1024x682.jpg)

### Description

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  7](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-7-1024x682.jpg)

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  8](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-8-1024x682.jpg)

They are large, crow-like with a long tail and coppery brown wings.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  9](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-9-1024x575.jpg)

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  10](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-10-1024x682.jpg)

The head is black, upper mantle and underside are black glossed with purple.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  11](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-11-1024x575.jpg)

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  12](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-12-1024x634.jpg)

The back and wings are chestnut brown. There are no pale shaft streaks on the coverts. The eyes are ruby red.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  5](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-5-1024x575.jpg)

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  14](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-14-1024x682.jpg)

Juveniles are duller black with spots on the crown and there are whitish bars on the underside and tail. Sexes are similar in plumage, but females are larger than males.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  15](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-15-1024x682.jpg)

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  16](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-161-1024x682.jpg)

### Habitat

Found in wide range of coffee habitats from jungle to cultivation and urban gardens.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  29](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-29-1024x682.jpg)

Especially common in places like bamboo thickets.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  30](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-30-1024x682.jpg)

### Behaviour

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  31](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-31-1024x682.jpg)

The crow pheasant has a very distinctive call and can be heard at all hours of the day.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  32](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-32-1024x682.jpg)

At times two birds synchronize their call and the entire coffee forest resonates with the call.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  33](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-33-1024x682.jpg)

Generally they are seen in pairs but are highly territorial. They sunbathe in the early mornings singly or in pairs on the top of vegetation with their wings spread out.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  34](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-34-1024x682.jpg)

The territory of a nesting pair has been found in southern India to be 0.9 to 7.2 ha. They are most active in the warm hours of the morning and in the late afternoon.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  35](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-35-1024x575.jpg)

### Diet

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  36](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-36-1024x682.jpg)

The choice of food of the crow pheasant is its strength.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  37](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-37-1024x682.jpg)

The diet includes Insects, caterpillars, moths, larvae, small reptiles, eggs of small birds, snails, Frogs, grasshoppers, beetles.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  38](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-38-1024x682.jpg)

### Breeding

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  49](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-49-1024x697.jpg)

The Indian Crow Pheasant build their own nests. It is usually situated in the midst of some impenetrable thicket. The breeding season is after the [monsoon](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsoon) in southern India but varies in other parts of its range but chiefly June to September.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  50](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-50-1024x717.jpg)

Greater coucals are monogamous, and the courtship display involves chases on the ground and the male brings food gifts for the female.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  41](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-41-1024x682.jpg)

The female lowers her tail and droops her wings to signal acceptance.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  42](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-42-1024x682.jpg)

### Nesting

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  43](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-431-1024x682.jpg)

The nest is built mostly by the male over about three to eight days. The nest is a deep cup with a dome in dense vegetation inside tangles of creepers, bamboo clump or *Pandanus* crowns.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  44](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-44-1024x682.jpg)

They can be built as high as 6m above the ground and the typical clutch is 3–5 eggs. The eggs (of size 36–28 mm weighing 14.8 g ) are chalky white with a yellow glaze when laid that wears off.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  45](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-45-1024x682.jpg)

Both the male and the female take part in nest building. They lay 2 to 4 eggs that hatch after 15–16 days of incubation. The chicks take 18–22 days to fledge.

![2014 ecofriendlycoffee Dr.ATPSlide-  46](2014-ecofriendlycoffee-Dr.ATPSlide-46-1024x682.jpg)
